The ecosystem · Life of the inlet

The living web of the inlet

The Ecosystem Team maps what is alive along this stretch of Burrard Inlet — who eats whom, what shelters in the kelp, and how a single warming summer ripples up the food web. The infographic below gathers it into one picture.

Ecosystem Team · Field infographic · Open full size ↗

Reading the web

Nothing on this shore stands alone. The kelp feeds the grazers, the grazers feed the fish, the fish feed the birds and seals — and every link is only as strong as the water it lives in. The infographic traces those connections so the whole system can be seen at once.

Pull one thread and the rest move. A warmer summer thins the kelp; the grazers thin with it; the chain carries the loss upward long after the heat has passed.

Why it matters here

Burrard Inlet is a working harbour and a living habitat at the same time. The pressures the team tracks — warming water, runoff, lost eelgrass — don't announce themselves. They show up as absences: fewer fish, quieter tide pools, emptier shallows.

Mapping the ecosystem is how those absences become visible before they become permanent.
