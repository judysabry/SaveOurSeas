City planners · A proposal

Protecting Ambleside from the rising sea

Sea level rise is no longer a distant forecast — it is a planning problem with a deadline. As a waterfront community on Burrard Inlet, Ambleside faces flooding, shoreline erosion, and the slow loss of the public spaces that make it home. This is the City Planners Team's proposal: six ways to act while there is still time.

The proposed solutions

One shoreline, defended six ways

No single wall holds back a rising ocean. A resilient Ambleside layers its defences — hardening what must be hard, softening where nature can do the work, and preparing the spaces and routes the community depends on every day.

🌊 Solution 01

Strengthen waterfront protection

The seawall and other shoreline protections should be strengthened and raised in the areas most vulnerable to flooding. Acting now reduces future damage and protects the community spaces that line the waterfront.

🌿 Solution 02

Restore natural barriers

Wetlands, coastal plants, and green spaces absorb excess water and slow erosion. Expanding and protecting these areas is a sustainable way to defend the shore — one that benefits local wildlife at the same time.

🏞️ Solution 03

Adapt parks & recreation

Fields and parks near the ocean should be readied for future flooding. Better drainage, elevated surfaces, and flood-resistant design can keep these places safe and usable during extreme weather.

🏗️ Solution 04

Upgrade infrastructure

Roads, storm drains, utilities, and public buildings should be upgraded to withstand higher water and stronger storms. Future development should be designed with climate resilience built in from the start.

🛣️ Solution 05

Protect transportation routes

Marine Drive and the surrounding roads are essential connections. Protecting them from flooding must be a priority — both for everyday transportation and for emergency access when storms hit.

🤝 Solution 06

Involve the community

Sea level rise asks for cooperation from residents, businesses, schools, organizations, and government. Public education and community planning help ensure everyone plays a role in preparing for what's ahead.

Conclusion

There is still time to prepare

Sea level rise is a significant challenge for Ambleside — but it is not an inevitability we have to accept unprepared. By strengthening shoreline protection, restoring natural barriers, adapting public spaces, upgrading infrastructure, and involving the community, Ambleside can reduce future risk and protect the waterfront for generations to come. Acting now is how we build a safer, more resilient shore in the years ahead.
