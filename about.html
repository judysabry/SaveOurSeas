The story · Why this exists

A small record of a changing coast

Ambleside Shore is an independent project about one stretch of the Burrard Inlet shoreline — the water, the names carried here long before us, and the slow pressure that is reshaping it. It is not a campaign or an institution. It is a record, made so a familiar coast is harder to ignore.

Where it began

It started with a camera on a wet morning and a question that wouldn't settle: how do you show a place changing slowly enough that nobody notices? The films came first — short, unhurried looks at Ambleside under rain, tide, and a warming sky.

The website grew around them, gathering what a single video can't hold: the place names, the everyday habits behind the change, and a few games that let you feel the pressure instead of just reading about it.

What it's for

The coast doesn't need another lecture. It needs to be looked at — closely, repeatedly, by the people who walk it. This project tries to make that easy and a little beautiful.

Everything here points the same direction: pay attention to this shoreline, learn the names it already carries, and notice how ordinary choices ripple out to meet the water.
